History of the zoo

Just for some background information, the first zoo in Singapore was established within Singapore Botanic Gardens in 1875 before it closed in 1905 due to shortage of funding as well as high animal death rates. The Singapore Zoo officially opened on 27th June 1973 by the then Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Defence Goh Keng Swee.The zoo had an initial collection of around 300 animals housed in about 50 enclosures. Animals mostly came from the Southeast Asian region but there were also animals sourced from other parts of the world.

Food and beverages

Make sure you don't go hungry and thirsty by knowing where the food and beverage outlets are. The Zoo caters to everyone with a wide variety of food. Two animal themed food outlets, the famous Ah Meng Restaurant, named after the famous orang utan Ah Meng, a special part of the Singapore Zoo's history. The Inuka cafe is also another cafe catering to local cuisines. For those who love their fast food, the Zoo also has McDonalds and KFC outlets inside, providing something for everyone to ensure the day is complete with good food along with the great activities.
